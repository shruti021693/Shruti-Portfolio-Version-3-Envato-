Greater Than Zero Percent

Background

Greater Than Zero Percent (GTZP) is non-profit that finds people and causes/orgnizations changing the world and sharing their stories to collect donations for them.

Problem

How might we get users to intuitively find, learn, connect, and engage/donate with a charity?

Solution

The restructuring and remodelling of the website would be helpful in making clear navigation to their donors and easy for them to learn about different organizations and help them through donations.

Discover

Research

After analyzing the old website, we listed pain points that helped focus on survey questions for the audience.

  • Gain an understanding of what GTZP is/purpose
  • Find/learn about a charity you might want to donate to
  • Donate to a charity (a moderator will stop before purchase)
  • Buy a product from the website(please don't enter real personal info)
Affinity Diagram

Synthesized information from research with team members from over 120+ data points to 8 classifications, and 3 main themes to identify the top pain points and focus items from the usability study.

Purpose/Motivation
-Interviewees liked knowing who is behind what they are giving their money toward
-People are concerned about the “why” of organizations Accessing the mission statement quickly on each charity creates purpose for donation
Clarity
-Interviewees will do the least amount of content consumption possible to complete a task
-Interviewees get overwhelmed with many different types of formats and information given on one-page Current design results in interviewees to feeling confused and overwhelmed
Connection
-People want to know what their donations are going towards When shown ideas similar to their own they connect more with causes/efforts

Define

Persona

Their potential visitors will use their website to learn more about services such as what to expect, times, and locations. The Average donor in the US is 64 years old and makes 2 charitable donations a year. Among new donors and donors who gave more since March of 2020, young people and donors of color showed up more prominently than older generations and white donors. (blackbaud institute)

Wireframe Usability Test
Findings:

Confusion over the checkout and people not understanding how to complete the check out.
issues finding browse organizations on the homepage.
Wanted confirmation of receipt.
Users want purpose/motivation, clarity, and connection.
Wanted to be lead to the browse organizations not the homepage after purchase.
Wanted confirmation of receipt.

Major Changes:

-Added visual/symbolic benefits of donation of the organization to show the impact and improve credibility
-Added receipt confirmation at end of purchase.
-Changed user flow to go to browse organizations after purchase (not homepage).
-Broke the checkout page into two pages (that look identical) with a progress bar at top.
-Moved the purchase gear section off the primary navigation
and added a bag for donations/purchases and a profile.
