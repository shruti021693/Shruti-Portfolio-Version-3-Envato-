App Redesigning Projects

Background

As a UX designer, I have always been passionate about creating products that are intuitive, efficient, and enjoyable for users.

I have been using these apps for several years and I addressed a couple of issues over certain features. I decided to use my skills and abilities to address them. I saw this as an exciting opportunity to make a real difference for the app's users.

My goal was to create user-centered design solutions that were simple and thoughtful, while also adding new functionality that would make the app more valuable for users. To do this, I began by addressing the usability issues and collecting feedback and complaints from my friends and colleagues. I found that 95% of them agreed on the same problems they were experiencing and shared their ideas for potential solutions.

Based on this data, I came up with a list of design solutions for the app features. I sketch out wireframes and prototypes to explore different ideas and tested them with them. By redesigning the features, I utilized a human-centered design mentality to solve usability issues, streamline the user interface, and add updates to functionality that meet the users needs and goals.

Problem

It can be difficult to confirm/check the selected destination after starting the route.

Pain Point

  • - Destination name is not visible on the route screen
  • - Lots of clicks to confirm the destination name
  • - Difficult to operate it while driving

How might users are able to see the destination name on the screen of selected route.
Problem

Viewing other people's transaction activity on the home page of Venmo is unnecessary and distracting.

Pain Point

  • - Not intrested in seeing friend’s activity on the main page
  • - To view user’s transaction history and balance, user need to click the 'me' icon.
  • - Wanted to see Venmo balance immediatly on the main screen

How might users view their transaction history and balance amount on the main screen
Problem

The caption field for saved reels is too small to clearly display at least five lines of text.

Pain Point

  • - The caption field is too small to easily read the text.
  • - Some options, like tagging people or adding a location, are not used as frequently by users. So that space should use for extending the caption field.

How might users view clear and long text with fewer distractions on Instagram’s reel editing page.
Problem

Creating a single note that combines checkboxes and additional notes, as well as styling the text, can be challenging.

Pain Point

  • - Unable to create a categarised checklist
  • - Can not create a single note that contain multiple types of writing notes (eg. checklist, table, etc.)
  • - Difficult to manage, observe the different work checklists
  • - Can not place header to checklist and unable to style the text (color, thickness, etc.)

How might users create a combined note, checkbox and styles of the text.
Conclusion

While working on this project, I realized that redesigning app features is a complex process that requires a deep understanding of users needs, a innovative experiments, and a willingness to iterate and improve.

I was thrilled to have the opportunity to work on improving the experiences of the products, and I knew that the redesign would be a valuable addition to my portfolio as a UX designer. It demonstrated my ability to analyze and understand the functionality of the product in relation to user needs, and to create designs that were intuitive, efficient, and visually appealing.
